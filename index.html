Why this structure

Built like a case file — observe, test, then specialise.

The programme is developed as per National Commission for Allied and Healthcare Professions (NCAHP) guidelines, and it's sequenced deliberately: foundations first, research tools next, then real clinical and applied exposure through labs, internships and a final dissertation.

Yr 1

Foundations

General, biological and Indian psychology, personality theory, and your first lab work — learning to observe before you interpret.

Yr 2

Method & Mechanism

Statistics, research methodology, cognitive and abnormal psychology, assessment and testing — the toolkit every psychologist needs.

Yr 3

Applied Fields

Neuropsychology, forensic psychology, rehabilitation and sports psychology, plus your first internship — theory meets the field.

Yr 4

Practice & Dissertation

Clinical psychology, crisis intervention, psychotherapy, apprenticeship and a 12-credit dissertation to close the case.
